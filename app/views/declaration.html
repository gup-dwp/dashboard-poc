  • you’ll pay back any money you’re overpaid if you’re asked to

Alternative pattern

Use this when:

  • the user needs to make another interaction (eg to give their consent for the DWP to contact people they’ve listed)

Consent and declaration

Do you agree to the Carer’s Allowance unit contacting anyone mentioned in this form?

By sending the application you agree that:

  • the information is correct and complete as far as you know
  • you’ll pay back any money you’re overpaid if you’re asked to
  • you’ll report changes of circumstances or those of the person you care for promptly either online or by contacting the Carer’s Allowance Unit.
You could be prosecuted or have to pay a financial penalty if you deliberately give the wrong or incomplete information or don’t report changes. Your Carer’s allowance may also be stopped or reduced.

Content notes

  • Refer to the user in the second person apart from in the action text.
  • Use bullets introduced by the text ‘By sending this [application] you agree:’ and list any conditions of applying.
  • If reporting changes is required this should give an example of what and explain how.
  • Use links for explanatory content - the declaration only has to give the user opportunity to read terms, not contain them itself.
  • The number of conditions may vary - only list the conditions that apply to your service (5 standard messages are given in the examples).
  • Any other content should be placed above the legal declaration.
  • The risks and consequences should include the warning call out style (an exclamation mark).
  • Avoid the word ‘prosecute’ unless you get specific legal advice otherwise.
  • Don’t use a check box - the action button represents positive action and acceptance.
  • The text of the action button should be in the first person ‘I agree’.
